— Google Go North

Go North is an initiative launched by Google to provide a rallying point for the emerging Canadian tech industry. The first two Go North events were attended by more than 600 of the most influential players in the Canadian tech space including Justin Trudeau, Eric Schmidt, Demis Hassabis (co-founder of DeepMind), Garret Camp (co-founder of Uber), Ted Livingston (Founder of Kik), David McKay (President of RBC), Navdeep Bains (Canadian Minister of Innovation, Science and Economic Development) among others. #GoNorth was one of the top trending hashtags in Canada for the better part of both event days.

I developed the core brand motifs and logo for the initiative. Joel Tellier and I created the final mark. Underline Studio, Ben Ruby, Ralph Damman and I also created printed material and web assets for the first event.

Go North event

Challenge

We were given this brief by Google: Distill the identity of Canadian tech into a single mark and aesthetic that encompasses this:

A rallying cry. Bringing together Canadians at home and abroad under a common banner as new frontiersmen. Brave entrepreneurs pushing the limits of the possible, and united in the singular pursuit of discovery. Charting a course for the unwritten tomorrow. Drawing from our history to achieve a greater future. Bold - passionate - innovative - resourceful - driven - aggressive. Give the Canadian startup/innovation community a branding identity that they can be proud of.

Solution

I decided to use a topographical map as the main feature of the identity. A topographical map represents exploration and discovery but also elevation and ascension. A topographical map is symbolically historical but aesthetically modern. I loved this project as it gave me the opportunity to re-envision what it means to be Canadian in the tech industry.
